Our Mission

Professional and advancing student musicians replace their strings regularly to ensure they get the best possible sound. At the same time, musicians in other parts of the world and in under-resourced areas of the U.S. have difficulty acquiring quality strings. Strings are expensive!

Sostenuto Strings collects used or spare violin, viola, cello, and bass strings from professional/youth orchestra, luthiers, and conservatories and donates them to community music programs in the U.S. and abroad. “Sostenuto” means to sustain a note beyond its original value. By donating and distributing quality strings, we can extend their value and enrich the lives of others as they make music.

About Sostenuto Strings

Hi, I’m Josiah Kam. I’m a cellist at the Manhattan School of Music PreCollege.

When I was younger, I practiced on a cello with a poor sound. The C string in particular was frustrating because it seemed to resist my every command instead of obeying. I voiced my complaints to my teacher, and because I could not get a better cello at the time, he recommended better strings. The new strings yielded a much improved sound, which in turn strengthened my resolve to practice. My hope is that Sostenuto Strings will empower others similarly in their musical pursuits.
